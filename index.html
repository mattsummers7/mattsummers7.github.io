Haptic Feedback Dissertation

In the final year of my degree my dissertation was on haptic feedback and its impact on the players gameplay experience. For this project I worked in VR using the Unity XR Toolkit to create a level designed to test how much haptic feedback has an impact on player's immersion.

Technologies used:

Unity

WarLocks

WarLocks is a split-screen PVP multiplayer game created by TechJam. TechJam was a 13 person group of students who came together to create a game together. WarLocks allows players to pick one of four elemental wizards and battle it out using unique elemental spells and collectable ultimate abilities to fight other players in a variety of different maps.

Technologies used:

Unity

Teams

GitHub


Here is a gameplay trailer that shows off WarLocks and some of the exciting classes and abilities available to play with.



In this collaborative project, our team were focused on creating an engaging, multiplayer experience.

My primary responsibilities were developing player movement, camera controls, and movement abilities. I designed and implemented, from scratch, custom player movement scripts from scratch using the New Unity Input System, allowing for fully adjustable speed and jump heights. I also created unique movement abilities for different player classes.

I was also supporting the team by brainstorming ideas to bring the game to life as well as supporting the team with bug fixes across the whole game.

VR Relaxation Puzzle Game

For this task I wanted to create a game that was both engaging and relaxing. I created a VR puzzle game in which players solve different puzzles to complete a constellation in space.

Technologies used:

Unity

Virtual Reality

GitHub


Designed to immerse players in a serene environment, this demo highlights the potential for creating calming and engaging VR experiences.



Through research I had the idea to create a VR puzzle experience that lets players calmly solve a puzzle to create constellation to decorate their hub space.

The experience so far includes three unique puzzles: a 'Simon Says' game, a search and find game and a laser destruction game.

The game makes full use of the XR Interaction Toolkit with grabbable objects and sockets as well multiple game managers that work together to provide a smooth and bug free player experience.
